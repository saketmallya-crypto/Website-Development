Science

In schools, the science stream is typically offered for Higher Secondary levels (Classes 11 and 12). It is designed to provide a foundational understanding of natural phenomena, technology, and analytical problem-solving. This stream is generally divided into two main branches: Medical (PCB) and Non-Medical (PCM).

Commerce

The Commerce stream in schools is designed for students interested in the world of business, trade, and financial systems. Unlike the Science stream, which focuses on natural laws, Commerce provides a deep dive into how economies function and how businesses are managed.

Humanities

The humanities stream, also commonly known as the Arts stream, focuses on the study of human society, culture, behavior, and thought. It is an intellectually rich and versatile choice for students interested in critical thinking, research, and understanding global institutions.

AI

In schools, an "AI stream" generally refers to the Artificial Intelligence subject offered as a specialized skill-based elective (Subject Code 843) for Classes 11 and 12. While not a standalone "stream" like Science or Commerce, it is often integrated into these streams to provide technical specialization in machine learning and data science.

Mass Media

In schools, Mass Media Studies is primarily offered as a vocational or skill-based elective subject (Subject Code 835) for Classes 11 and 12, rather than a completely separate "stream" like Science or Commerce. It is most commonly chosen by students in the Humanities/Arts stream, but can often be taken alongside Commerce or Science subjects depending on the school's flexibility.
